<p class="j1LEL va-er" data-mce-fragment="1" data-pm-slice="1 1 []" dir="auto" id="foo" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span data-mce-fragment="1">After sticking to the Pi Camera for a long time, finally, there seems to be a very promising alternative featuring twice the resolution and built-in autofocus capabilities: The Arducam 16MP camera. I got lucky being able to test this new camera...</span></p>
<p class="j1LEL va-er" data-mce-fragment="1" dir="auto" id="6e58s" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span data-mce-fragment="1"><a class="Y-s64 XzMaT" data-hook="WebLink" data-mce-fragment="1" data-mce-href="https://www.arducam.com/" href="https://www.arducam.com/" target="_blank"><u data-mce-fragment="1">Arducam </u></a>is a well-established open-source hardware specialist from China, who is currently running a <a class="Y-s64 XzMaT" data-hook="WebLink" data-mce-fragment="1" data-mce-href="https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/arducam/high-resolution-autofocus-camera-module-for-raspberry-pi" href="https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/arducam/high-resolution-autofocus-camera-module-for-raspberry-pi" target="_blank"><u data-mce-fragment="1">Kickstarter campaign</u></a> featuring a new 16 megapixel camera. The camera does not only exceed both the Pi Camera v2.1 and even the HQ camera in resolution, but also has built-in autofocus and comes at an amazing price of ~14&euro;, during the Kickstarter and ~22&euro; after the campaign. The camera is fully compatible with the Raspberry Pi, so I got super excited...</span></p>
<p class="j1LEL va-er" data-mce-fragment="1" dir="auto" id="6g3j3" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span data-mce-fragment="1">See the following comparison (source image in <a class="Y-s64 XzMaT" data-hook="WebLink" data-mce-fragment="1" data-mce-href="https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/arducam/high-resolution-autofocus-camera-module-for-raspberry-pi" href="https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/arducam/high-resolution-autofocus-camera-module-for-raspberry-pi" target="_blank"><u data-mce-fragment="1">Kickstarter campaign</u></a>)</span></p>
<div style="text-align: center;"><img alt="" src="/assets/img/posts/2021-12-09-openscan-arducam-testing-the-new-16-megapixel-camera/2021-12-01-arducam01-600x600.webp" style="float: none;"/></div>
<p class="j1LEL va-er" data-pm-slice="1 1 []" dir="auto" id="9ldfe" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span>A note on Crowdfunding campaigns - before taking part in any campaign as a backer, please read T&amp;Cs. Rewards aren&rsquo;t guaranteed! And it is very important to do your own research on the project and companies involved!</span></p>
<p class="j1LEL va-er" dir="auto" id="7q5j" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span>Anyway, as Arducam is a well-established company, I reached out and Lee immediately responded and shipped a sample. It took only three days between first mail and having the product in my hand, which is remarkable!</span></p>
<p class="j1LEL va-er" dir="auto" id="4qf19" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span>After I got the package, I immediately had to mount the camera to the <a class="Y-s64 XzMaT" data-hook="WebLink" data-mce-href="https://en.openscan.eu/openscan-mini" href="https://en.openscan.eu/openscan-mini" target="_blank"><u>OpenScan Mini</u></a> and everything fits perfectly as the footprint of the pcb is the same as the Pi Camera v2.1! Note, that the camera module is slightly thicker due to the autofocus mechanism.</span></p>
<p class="j1LEL va-er" dir="auto" id="5s1vl" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span>It became time to start coding, which I was a little afraid of... But following <a class="Y-s64 XzMaT" data-hook="WebLink" data-mce-href="https://www.arducam.com/docs/cameras-for-raspberry-pi/raspberry-pi-libcamera-guide/#how-to-install-libcamera-d9f38d46-8576-43e2-9375-e225c272095f" href="https://www.arducam.com/docs/cameras-for-raspberry-pi/raspberry-pi-libcamera-guide/#how-to-install-libcamera-d9f38d46-8576-43e2-9375-e225c272095f" target="_blank"><u>Arducams clear instructions</u></a>, it took only 10 minutes to create the first image! I continued playing around with the autofocus feature and testing some individual settings (like fixing exposure) and I became more and more excited as the overall image quality seemed superb.</span></p>
<div class="j1LEL va-er" dir="auto" indentation="0" style="text-align: center;" textstyle="[object Object]"><span><img data-mce-src="https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0810/2327/1257/files/2021-12-01_Arducam02_600x600.webp?v=1710325567" src="/assets/img/posts/2021-12-09-openscan-arducam-testing-the-new-16-megapixel-camera/2021-12-01-arducam02-600x600.webp" style="float: none;"/></span></div>

<p class="j1LEL va-er" dir="auto" id="9imri" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span>I used <a class="Y-s64 XzMaT" data-hook="WebLink" data-mce-href="https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:3962430" href="https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:3962430" target="_blank"><u>this 3d printed rook</u></a> scaled to 50mm height. This thing has so many tiny features! The difference in resolution is much more obvious when you look at some of those details, like the stairs:</span></p>

<p class="j1LEL va-er" data-pm-slice="1 1 []" dir="auto" id="6e7qe" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span>In order to use the camera with the OpenScan Mini, I quickly modified the firmware. It did not even take an hour to get the camera to run as part of the scan routine and show a preview in the browser interface. So, 3d scanning should reveal the full potential of this camera.</span></p>

<p class="j1LEL va-er" dir="auto" id="ful2s" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span>Conclusion:</span></p>
<p class="j1LEL va-er" dir="auto" id="c281n" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span>I will definitely incorporate the camera into the OpenScan Firmware. Both, the increased resolution and thus accuracy, and the autofocus of the camera, are two major improvements to the machine. Especially the OpenScan Classic could hugely benefit from the autofocus, as setting the focus of the pi camera v2.1 manually can be quite a pain ...</span></p>
