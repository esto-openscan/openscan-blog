<p class="j1LEL va-er" data-mce-fragment="1" data-pm-slice="1 1 []" dir="auto" id="foo" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span data-mce-fragment="1">This is probably one of the most famous sculptures in my town, and thus it needed to be scanned ;) I will try to collect as many digital souvenirs of various places, and maybe some of you want to join.</span></p>
<p class="j1LEL va-er" data-mce-fragment="1" dir="auto" id="aj6fi" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span data-mce-fragment="1">This is only one of a pair of lions that is situated in front of the central building of the Martin Luther University of Halle-Wittenberg, which is known to locals as the L&ouml;wengeb&auml;ude ('Lions Building'). They were created in 1822 for the Silesian town of Bunzlau (now Boles&lstrok;awiec) and moved to Halle (Saale) in 1868.</span></p>
<div class="j1LEL va-er" data-mce-fragment="1" dir="auto" indentation="0" style="text-align: center;" textstyle="[object Object]"><span data-mce-fragment="1"><img src="/assets/img/posts/2021-09-27-scantheworld-lion-sculpture-in-halle-saale/2021-09-22-scantheworld-lion-600x600.webp" style="float: none;"/></span></div>
<div class="j1LEL va-er" data-pm-slice="1 1 []" dir="auto" id="v4lh" indentation="0" style="text-align: center;" textstyle="[object Object]"><span><a class="Y-s64 XzMaT" data-hook="WebLink" href="https://skfb.ly/o79T7" target="_blank"><em><u>See and download the 3d model on Sketchfab</u></em></a> </span></div>
<p class="j1LEL va-er" dir="auto" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="j1LEL va-er" dir="auto" id="cj5ap" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span>Scanning cast iron surfaces is usually quite a challenge, but I am happy, that the OpenScanCloud engine (<a class="Y-s64 XzMaT" data-hook="WebLink" href="https://github.com/OpenScanEu/OpenScanCloud" target="_blank"><u>see Github</u></a><u>)</u> did a wonderful job with those 129 photos (Iphone XS). The only noteworthy 'setting' is, that I waited for a fully overcast day and thus diffuse lighting.</span></p>
<p class="j1LEL va-er" dir="auto" id="fh3a9" indentation="0" textstyle="[object Object]"><span>See the following video for the image series. Note how I moved the camera between each shot (in contrary to panoramic shots)</span></p>

<div data-breakout="normal">
<p class="_9-O5y h4vUg KNByY iPC6I TI-xX" id="viewer-ar2pe"><span class="_1N6tE"><span>The goal is to capture each spot on at least three images. With difficult surfaces like this one, it is usually a good idea to overshoot, and I think, doubling the image number could have increased the resulting quality even more.</span></span></p>
